A Mid-level View of Hong Kong Island in the Qing Dynasty
Oct 13, 2021

我的这篇收藏拍摄于香港太平山半山处，是极为少见的清代全景蛋白俯拍照（25.4*20.32 cm)。当时在半山通行的多为洋人,只有极少部分获得许可的华工。照片的角度真实的呈现出当时香港的华夷杂处，欧亚合璧。
This collection of mine was taken in the mid-levels of Victoria Peak, Hong Kong. It is an extremely rare panoramic protein overhead photograph of the Qing Dynasty (25.4*20.32 cm). At that time, most of the people passing through the Mid-Levels were foreigners, and only a few Chinese workers were permitted. The angle of the photo truly shows that the Chinese and Barbarian places in Hong Kong at that time were a fusion of Europe and Asia.

Panoramic view

Victoria Harbor维多利亚港，位于九龙半岛与港岛之间，举世闻名。此港水深且航道宽阔，所以早年被英国人看中，有发展成东亚第一港口的潜力，亦将为其在远东的贸易做出不可或缺的贡献。
Victoria Harbor Victoria Harbor, located between the Kowloon Peninsula and Hong Kong Island, is world-famous. The port has deep water and wide navigation channels, so it was regarded by the British in the early years. It has the potential to develop into the first port in East Asia, and it will also make an indispensable contribution to its trade in the Far East.

初期的维港西侧没有像现在地图标记的那么远，只包括港岛北角，九龙红磡及昂船洲水域。清末已有渔船，邮轮，商船及各类舢板往返于南北两岸，交织出繁华的景象。

In the early days, the west side of Victoria Harbour was not as far as the map now marked, and only included the waters of North Point on Hong Kong Island, Kowloon Hung Hom and Stonecutters Island. At the end of the Qing Dynasty, fishing boats, cruise ships, merchant ships and all kinds of immortal boards travelled back and forth between the north and the south, creating a prosperous scene.

Since the beginning of the twentieth century, Hong Kong’s shipbuilding industry has been very developed. The largest docks are located in Side Quarry Bay (belonging to Swire Pacific) and Hongci (belonging to Whampoa Dockyard).
