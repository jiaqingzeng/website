我的这张收藏是民国时期香港皇后大道的银盐照（11.16.7cm)。皇后大道（Queen’s Road)是香港开埠之后所建的第一条主要道路，亦是香港最早的填海工程，位于香港岛。分为皇后大道西、皇后大道中及皇后大道东，由中西区石塘咀延伸至湾仔区跑马地，最初是在1841年以移山的方式建成，通车于1842年2月，全长约5公里。
This collection of mine is a silver salt photo (11.16.7cm) from Queen’s Road in Hong Kong during the Republic of China. Queen’s Road (Queen’s Road) is the first major road built after the opening of Hong Kong as a port. It is also the earliest reclamation project in Hong Kong. It is located on Hong Kong Island. It is divided into Queen’s Road West, Queen’s Road Central and Queen’s Road East. It extends from Shek Tong Tsui in Central and Western District to Happy Valley in Wan Chai District. kilometer.

Queen’s Road是为了纪念当时的英国维多利亚女王（香港另有一条英皇道King’s Road,纪念英王乔治五世）。它的中文名本应译作“女王大道”，但被当时的在港华人错译为“皇后大道”；“后”在远古有君王之意，但近代中国不接受女皇帝。港英政府于1890年澄清错处，但道路的中文名还是将错就错沿用至今。香港日治时期，皇后大道曾被改名为“明治通”。
Queen’s Road is to commemorate Queen Victoria of England at the time (there is another King’s Road in Hong Kong to commemorate King George V). Its Chinese name should have been translated as “Queen’s Avenue”, but it was incorrectly translated as “Queen’s Avenue” by the Chinese in Hong Kong at that time. “Hou” meant king in ancient times, but modern China did not accept female emperors. The British Hong Kong government clarified the mistake in 1890, but the Chinese name of the road is still wrong and it is still used today.During the Japanese Occupation of Hong Kong, Queen’s Road was renamed “Meiji Street”.
